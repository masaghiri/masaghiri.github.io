Biography

Dr. Saghiri has a Ph.D. in Dental Material and is the Director of Lab of Biomaterial at Rutgers School of Dental Medicine (RSDM). He has broad expertise in the field of dental material with more than 100 publications in areas such as dental biomaterials, endodontic cements, restorative materials, oral biology, and angiogenesis. He is the inventor of Nano White Mineral Trioxide Aggregate (NWMTA), which is a US patented novel endodontic cement used as root canal filling biomaterial. In addition, Dr. Saghiri is the only researcher in the dental clinic in the East Coast to receive the Denbur Award Tech. Dr. Saghiri was nominated because he is a Clinician, Researcher and an Entrepreneur. He has worked with high-tech companies including RooTogen and Philips Oral Healthcare. He has close to a 100 articles indexed in PubMed, 61 of which he is the first author for, a number of patents, and diverse collaborations. AFSAR LAB is currently working on the expandable Endodontics sealer project. This project was patented and has been made possible by Research to Prevent Blindness to the Department of Ophthalmology and Visual Sciences; the Retina Research Foundation; and National Institutes of Health grants. Commonly used endodontic sealers used to fill the root canal space have two main limitations: shrinkage, which results in detachment of filling materials from the root canal walls, soluble, and/or “washing-out” of endodontic sealers. Both shrinkage and washing-out of sealers can cause gap formation inside and between the filling material and root canal walls. This gap formation can result in microbial colonization and reinfection of tooth root canal space, which in turns can lead to the failure of the treatment. This sealer is an innovation on the sealers on the market because it has a unique ability to expand instead of shrink. This would eliminate the cause of most endodontic failure. Thus, creating a safer, easier, quicker, and more affordable procedure that can be performed by more providers to a wider range of patients.

Dr. Saghiri's Three Lines of Research: Dentin Vaccine, Application of Artificial Intelligence in Dentistry and Medicine, and Dental Biomaterials Manufacturing

Dentin Vaccine: Dentin vaccine is a promising approach in the field of dental care that aims to strengthen and fortify dentin, the calcified tissue that forms the bulk of the tooth beneath the enamel. Dr. Saghiri's research in this area involves developing safe and effective crystallography-based treatments for dentin repair, using the latest advances in nanotechnology and materials science. Dentin vaccine induces internal stresses in the crystal lattice of the dentin apatite, which could improve the strength and durability of the dentin at the grain and grain boundary levels. Dr. Saghiri's research in this area has the potential to offer a more conservative and less invasive approach to tooth repair. Currently, dental restorations often involve removing damaged or decayed tooth material and replacing it with artificial materials. With Dentin Vaccine, however, it may be possible to repair and strengthen damaged teeth without the need for such invasive procedures. Another potential benefit is that it could help to prevent further damage to teeth that have already undergone restorative procedures. By strengthening the underlying dentin structure, Dentin Vaccine could reduce the risk of further damage and help to extend the lifespan of restorations. For those who are interested in Dentin Vaccine or would like to join Dr. Saghiri's research team, he is always looking for motivated individuals to collaborate with. Dr. Saghiri's research in the application of artificial intelligence (AI) in dentistry and medicine involves developing AI algorithms and tools that can assist clinicians in diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient management. By leveraging machine learning and deep learning techniques, Dr. Saghiri's research aims to improve the accuracy and efficiency of clinical decision-making, reduce treatment variability, and enhance patient outcomes. One of Dr. Saghiri's ongoing projects in this area involves developing an AI system that can automatically detect and classify dental caries (cavities) on dental radiographs. This system has the potential to significantly improve the accuracy and consistency of dental caries diagnosis, which is currently heavily reliant on clinician expertise and subjective interpretation of radiographic images. Dental Biomaterials Manufacturing:
Dr. Saghiri's research in dental biomaterials manufacturing involves developing novel dental materials and technologies that can improve the clinical performance, biocompatibility, and longevity of dental restorations. Dr. Saghiri's research team works on designing and manufacturing dental materials such as dental composites and adhesives that are resistant to wear, fracture, and degradation. One of Dr. Saghiri's ongoing projects in this area involves the development of a novel composite material that combines the strength and durability of ceramics with the flexibility and ease of use of composites. This material has the potential to significantly improve the longevity and performance of dental restorations while also reducing the need for invasive procedures and preserving healthy tooth structure.
Novel Expandable Endodontic Biomaterial

Dr. Saghiri's research has also focused on the development of new techniques for dental implant placement, including the use of novel biomaterials and tissue engineering approaches. These techniques have the potential to improve the success rates of dental implants, while also reducing the risk of complications and adverse reactions.
Moreover, Dr. Saghiri's research has had a direct correlation with the development of new products and technologies that have the potential to create jobs in the United States. For example, his work on dental composites has led to the development of new products by dental companies that have the potential to create new jobs in research, development, manufacturing, and marketing. Similarly, his work on tissue engineering and biomaterials has the potential to create new jobs in the biotechnology and medical device industries.
The merit of Dr. Saghiri's research is widely recognized, with numerous publications in prestigious scientific journals, as well as numerous awards and honors. He has also served as a reviewer for several leading scientific journals, contributing to the advancement of research in the field of dentistry.
